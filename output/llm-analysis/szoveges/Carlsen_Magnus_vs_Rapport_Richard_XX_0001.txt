The game kicks off with Magnus Carlsen steering into the Caro-Kann Defense after pawn to e4 followed by Richard Rapport responding with pawn to c6. This opening is often known for its solid structure and slight concession of the center but offers Black a durable setup. White immediately claims the center with pawn to d4 and Black confronts it with pawn to d5. The exchange on d5 allows both players a solid foothold in the center as Carlsen develops with bishop to d3. However, the early h3 by White is a somewhat unusual measure, neither aggressive nor defensive.

The opening unwinds as Black develops knight to f6, and after several exchanges, Carlsen advances with both queenside and kingside minor pieces. At move eight, Carlsen's queen takes position on e2, and Rapport's knight boldly advances to e4. As White pushes bishop to b5, launching a check, Rapport simply shields with bishop to d7, further reinforcing his d5 pawn. Carlsen now strains to find control over the board as he shifts his knight to f3. Quickly, however, Rapport castles, securing his king's safety.

In the middlegame, White starts applying pressure with knight to g5, but Black's response with knight to f4 causes White to reconsider. Carlsen works his queen over to f3 to combat Black's threats, yet Rapport weaves his queen to b5. Not deterred, Magnus initiates a pawn storm with g3 to undermine Black's knights. Despite this, Rapport maintains his position with pawn to f6. The exchange of pawns goes on with White picking up Black's bishop pawns by force.

As momentum shifts, Carlsen misses critical defenses and suddenly White is on the back foot. White's move, queen to f1, stifles his queen's activity, a major oversight. With Black grabbing White's queenside pawn on b2, Magnus makes the critical mistake of rook to d2, overlooking Black's looming threats. Black seizes the opportunity, reinforcing his position and increasing the tactical pressure. This was the major turning point as Carlsen finds himself significantly down in material and initiative. The lesson here: maintaining piece activity is crucial, especially when calculating defense against a looming attack.
